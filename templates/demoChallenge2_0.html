Demo Challenge #2: Spy Games.

For this next challenge, we’re going to take a look at the murky world of encryption.

People have been trying to hide messages for centuries - the earliest known use of cryptography is found in non-standard hieroglyphs carved into the wall of a tomb from the Old Kingdom of Egypt circa 1900 BCE.

These are not thought to be serious attempts at secret communications, however, but rather to have been attempts at mystery, intrigue, or even amusement for literate onlookers.

One of the simplest ciphers is a Caesar Cipher, where each letter in the alphabet is replaced by a letter a fixed number of positions down the alphabet, so with a right shift of 3, ‘a’ becomes ‘d’, ‘b’ becomes ‘e’ and so forth.

So, “I love you” becomes, “L oryi brx” 

Don’t worry … we’re going to go with something even simpler!
